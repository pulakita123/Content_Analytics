# Overview
This project analyzes how a digital content library has evolved over time using an interactive analytics dashboard. The goal is to provide clear visibility into long-term trends in content growth, format distribution, and geographic contribution, enabling stakeholders to move from assumption-based decisions to evidence-based insights.
The project follows a Business Analystâ€“driven approach, starting from business problem identification and requirement definition, and ending with measurable insights delivered through data visualization.

# Business Problems
Limited visibility into year-by-year growth of the content library
Difficulty understanding how the balance between movies and series has changed over time
Lack of clarity around geographic and genre-level contribution
Static reports that do not support exploratory analysis

# Business Questions
How has the content library grown over the years?
Is there a visible shift in content format across time?
Which countries and genres contribute most to new content?
How do content trends change when filtered by time or geography?

# KPIs Defined
Total titles added
Year-wise content growth
Content distribution by format (movies vs series)
Country and genre contribution

# Stakeholders
Content Strategy Team
Business Operations Team
Senior Management

# Dashboard Features
Interactive Power BI dashboard
Clustered horizontal bar charts for year-wise comparison
Trend charts to visualize growth patterns
Pie charts to show content composition
Table for detailed record-level analysis
Slicers for year, content type, country, and genre
All visuals respond dynamically to user selections, allowing flexible analysis without additional reports.

# Key Insights
Content additions increased steadily in later years
Series content grew faster compared to movies over time
Content sourcing became more geographically diverse
Insights are based purely on observed data trends, without external assumptions or causal claims.

# Assumptions & Constraints
Analysis is based on content addition dates, not performance or engagement metrics
No revenue or viewership data is included
Findings represent trends, not impact or causation

# Tools Used
Power BI
Structured dataset (content metadata)

# Outcome
This project demonstrates how business problems can be translated into analytical requirements and visual insights. It highlights core Business Analyst skills such as requirement mapping, KPI definition, stakeholder-focused reporting, and data-driven storytelling.
